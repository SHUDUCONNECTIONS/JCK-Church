About the Programme

The JCK Life Changers Farming Project was established to address food insecurity in Mpumalanga communities by teaching people to grow their own food. We believe that food sovereignty — the ability of a community to feed itself — is one of the most powerful forms of freedom.

We work with community members of all ages, providing agricultural training, seeds, tools, and ongoing mentorship. The food produced is used to feed less fortunate families in the area, with surplus crops sold to build economic independence for participating households.

What We Do

  • Provide agricultural training workshops for community members
  • Supply seeds, seedlings, and basic farming tools
  • Mentor community farmers throughout the growing season
  • Distribute surplus produce to less fortunate families
  • Help farmers access local markets to sell their crops
  • Run a school farming component ensuring no child goes hungry

Why It Matters

Hunger is not just a food problem — it is a dignity problem. When a family cannot put food on the table, it affects every part of their lives: children's ability to learn, parents' ability to work, and the community's sense of hope. By teaching people to grow their own food, we give communities lasting power over their own lives.

Our school farming programme runs alongside the main project, ensuring that children at partner schools have access to fresh produce and that no child has to study on an empty stomach.

Our Approach

We believe in hands-on, community-led development. We do not simply hand out food — we equip people with the skills to produce food themselves. Our agricultural trainers work alongside community members on their own plots, building confidence and knowledge season by season.
