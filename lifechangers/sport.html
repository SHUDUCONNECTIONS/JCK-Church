About the Programme

Sport for Change is JCK Life Changers' youth and community empowerment programme, using structured sports activities as a vehicle for personal development, social cohesion, and skill building. We believe that sport is never just about the game — it is always about the person playing it.

In Mamelodi, idle time is dangerous time for young people. Unemployment, substance abuse, and gang activity are real threats to young people who have no structured environment to channel their energy and potential. Sport for Change provides that environment — and more.

What We Do

  • Organise regular structured sports days and tournaments
  • Provide coaching and mentorship from experienced volunteers
  • Integrate life skills workshops alongside sports activities
  • Create pathways to formal sports clubs and career opportunities
  • Include elderly community members in appropriate physical activities
  • Use sport as a bridge for cross-community interaction and peacebuilding

Life Skills Through Sport

Every sport teaches something valuable. Team sports teach communication, trust, and shared purpose. Individual disciplines teach self-discipline, resilience, and goal-setting. Winning teaches celebration. Losing teaches character. We integrate these lessons explicitly — our coaches are life coaches as much as sports coaches.

We run workshops on topics including conflict resolution, goal setting, financial basics, and health — woven into the sporting programme so young people receive practical life preparation alongside physical development.

Including the Elderly

Sport for Change is not only for youth. We include elderly community members in appropriate physical activities, recognising that physical activity and social engagement are vital to healthy ageing. Walking groups, gentle exercise, and intergenerational sports events bring generations together — combating both youth idleness and elderly isolation at once.

Career Pathways

For young people who show sporting talent, we actively help connect them to formal sporting clubs, provincial opportunities, and bursary programmes. Sport should not be a dead end — it should be a doorway. We help youth see sport as a potential career and give them the support to pursue it.

Youth Empowerment

All Ages Welcome

From youth to elderly — our programme brings generations together through the universal language of sport and play.

Life Skills Integration

Every sports session is paired with practical life skills — because the goal is not a trophy, but a transformed life.
